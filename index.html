Excessive Speed Patterns in Sacramento

This is a simple look at where higher speeds show up across a few corridors. The focus is not on averages. It is on repeated high speed behavior.

Map

This map shows segments where higher speeds appear consistently.

Madison Ave

This corridor stood out immediately. Speeds stay high across multiple segments. This is not a freeway, and there are homes nearby. The way the road operates does not match the surroundings.

Munroe Street

This segment shows a different pattern. A few readings are much higher than expected. Even if they are not frequent, they are still important because of how high they are.

Q Street

Each segment looks normal on its own. Together, they show a consistent pattern across the corridor. This usually points to something about how the corridor is designed or used.

How this was built

Speed data was filtered to focus on higher values. Those readings were grouped by segment. The goal was to see where these patterns repeat.

Raw speed data → Filter high speeds → Group by segment → Map patterns

Takeaway

These patterns are not everywhere. They are concentrated in a few corridors. That makes them easier to understand and focus on.
